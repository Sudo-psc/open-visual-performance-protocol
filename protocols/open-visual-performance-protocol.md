# Open Visual Performance Protocol (OVPP)

**Version 2.1 — compatible with the book v2.9.13 claim contract.**

OVPP is an open readiness and feasibility protocol for screen-intensive work. It does not diagnose employees, prove productivity effects or promise financial return.

## Fixed boundaries

- Participation is voluntary and is never a performance criterion.
- Managers receive aggregated exposure, implementation and operational information only.
- Individual health data and clinical decisions remain in the authorized care pathway.
- Environmental measures and pause design do not delay referral. Severity, recurrence, functional impact, contact lenses, preference or red flags can trigger care in parallel.
- Use of an instrument requires an identified version, suitable validation, licensing and local clinical interpretation.

## Minimum package

Before collection, define sponsor, program owner, privacy lead, occupational-health pathway, population, purpose, lawful basis, retention, small-cell rule, instrument, operational comparator, quoted bill of materials, stop rules and day-90 decision.

## Ninety-day workflow

1. Readiness and governance review.
2. Aggregated baseline of symptoms and exposure.
3. Exposure and operational-priority map, never an individual clinical risk map.
4. Environmental standardization: screen distance around 40–70 cm, monitor position, glare, 40–60% humidity where controllable, 22–24 °C where controllable and airflow away from the face.
5. Pause and workflow infrastructure.
6. Parallel referral pathway.
7. Re-measurement with the same approved instrument and an independent operational indicator.
8. Decision: **stop**, **adapt**, or **advance to confirmatory evaluation**.

Primary outcomes are feasibility, participation, retention, completeness, adherence, acceptability, safety and implementation fidelity. Symptom and operational changes are exploratory unless the design has adequate units, power and a prespecified analysis.

## Economics

Before intervention:

- equivalent capacity = `N × direct_work_cost_in_horizon × symptomatic_fraction × perceived_limitation`;
- coverage threshold = `quoted_cost_in_same_horizon ÷ equivalent_capacity`.

Equivalent capacity is not cash and the threshold is not a probability of return. ROI remains unavailable until an incremental, attributable and auditable financial benefit and actual cost exist in the same horizon. Never convert a symptom score directly into money.

## Completion

A run is complete only when governance, implementation, missing data, events, referrals, costs, limitations, conflicts and a null-compatible decision memo are recorded. The protocol remains useful when the decision is to stop.
